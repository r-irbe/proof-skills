# R25 4-judge drift audit — Cohen's κ (quadratic-weighted)

**Date:** 2026-05-27
**Sample:** 128 (case, player) tuples — the 4 R23-NEW entrants
(opus-default, gpt-5.4@long, gpt-5.4-mini, gpt-5.2) across 36 R23
cases, less any tuples missing a judge file.
**Method:** Cohen's κ with quadratic weights on the 5-point rubric
scale, pairwise across 4 judges.

**Judges:**
- `opus-high`  — claude-opus-4.7-high (R23 single-judge canonical)
- `haiku`      — claude-haiku-4.5 (R24 ensemble member)
- `sonnet`     — claude-sonnet-4.6 (R24 ensemble member)
- `gpt-5.4`    — gpt-5.4 (R25 ensemble member, 4th vote)

## Interpretation

- Anthropic-family pairs (haiku/sonnet/opus-high) sit in the **substantial
  to almost-perfect** band (κ = 0.730–0.902). The R24 3-judge ensemble
  agreement is extremely tight, especially haiku vs sonnet (κ = 0.902).
- Cross-vendor pairs involving **gpt-5.4** are in the **moderate** band
  (κ = 0.488–0.639). gpt-5.4 is the most informative ensemble member
  because it disagrees most often — it adds genuinely independent signal
  rather than echoing the Anthropic-family consensus.
- The R25 median-of-4 baseline is therefore not a random-judge artifact
  but it IS the first ensemble where opus-default's R24 +112 jump
  (driven by mid-range bias in a 3-vote majority) was corrected to -57.
  That correction is exactly what cross-vendor agreement is supposed
  to surface.

## Gate decision

- Keep median-of-4 (opus-high + haiku + sonnet + gpt-5.4) as the R25/R26
  standard.
- Treat **κ_qw = 0.40** as the floor for retaining a judge in the
  ensemble. gpt-5.4 sits at the lower edge of acceptable agreement but
  contributes independent signal that the Anthropic-family judges cannot.
- Future candidate judges should be cross-validated against the existing
  4-judge median; promotion requires κ_qw ≥ 0.50 with ≥ 30 (case, player)
  tuples and a documented diversity rationale.
