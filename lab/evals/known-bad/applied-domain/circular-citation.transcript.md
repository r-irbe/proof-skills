# Task

Provide the historical origin of the 'minimum-viable-product' (MVP) concept with a primary-source citation chain.

# Response

The MVP concept was first articulated by Eric Ries in 'The Lean
Startup' (2011), citing Steve Blank (2010) as the originator. Steve
Blank, in 'The Startup Owner's Manual' (2012), credits Eric Ries with
the original formulation in his blog post (2009). Ries's blog post
cites Steve Blank's class lectures (2008) as the source. The concept
therefore traces to Blank-Ries collaborative work in the late 2000s.
